Spacing

"Spacing makes things clearer, cleaner and crisper. There are three aspects to consider: white space, line spacing and padding."

1

Use white space for clarity

White space refers to empty space on your website (which doesn't actually need to be white). Empty space may seem wasteful but actually it helps users.

Content with space around it looks more appealing and is easier to take in. You should look to space out elements, groups of elements and sections.

However, be careful not to exaggerate the gaps otherwise your site will start to look disjointed.

2

Define the hierarchy

White space describes invisible relationships between the elements of your website. You can help define the heirarchy of your site by using white space to separate sections.

You should define what you want you audience to see first and then establish a flow that corresponds to yur content's message. You can use white space to build that flow.

When well thought out white space will create distinct sections flowing from one to the next.
