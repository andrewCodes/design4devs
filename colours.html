Colours

"Color plays a vitally important role in the world in which we live. Color can sway thinking, change actions, and cause reactions."

1

Use only one base colour

This is a colour not including black, white or grey. I have used #FFB901 (Selective Yellow) as my base colour.

You will probably want to create light and dark versions of your base colour. I have created two light and two dark versions of my base colour.

Too many colours in too many places looks unpleasant and messy which is why we choose one.

2

Use a tool if you need more colours

You may find you need a selection of different colours for your website. If you do, use a tool to select your colour palette.

Using a tool to create your colour palette will ensure you do not use colours that clash or don't look good together.

I like to use Coolors but there are plenty of free tools available.

3

Use colour to draw attention

Colour can be a great way of making people take notice of certain parts of your website.

Not everything on your site is of equal importance. Using colour can highlight the more important parts.

It is a good idea to use colour to accentuate CTAs and buttons.

4

Avoid using black in your design

Black rarely occurs in the real world. The night sky... actually very dark blue. Your black dog... actually very dark brown.

Because of this, using black in your designs can look unnatural and a bit odd. Try a dark grey or other dark colour instead.

The black in my design? Actually a very dark indigo called Space Cadet (#2B2D42).

5

Use colours wisely

Colours can evoke emotional responses in people and have specific associations.

Ever wondered why Twitter, Facebook and LinkedIn all use blue as their base colour? It's because blue gives the impression something is trustworthy.

Red: power and excitement
Orange: cheerful and friendly
Yellow: energetic and happy
Green: nature and harmony
Blue: trustworthy and professional
Purple: nobilty and wealth
Pink: romance and love
Brown: reliability and relaxation
