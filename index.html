Typography

"The art and technique of arranging type to make written language readable and beautiful"

1

Use a font size between 15 and 25 pixels

For body text we need to make sure that our next is neither too small nor too large.

If we make our body text any smaller than 15 pixels becomes too hard to read. As a result of this, your text will fail accessibility tests and your site's search engine ranking will fall.

If we make body text any larger than 25 pixels it looks out of place.

2

Use really big font sizes for headings

There really is no size limit here. We are looking to grab the reader's attention.

When using very large font sizes you may find letters become blocky, too bold and don't look great. To address this, try reducing the font-weight.

You can give large letters a little more breathing space by increasing the letter-spacing.

3

Use line-spacing between 120% and 150%

Browser default line-spacing is 100%. However, this is too small for blocks of text.

If we use the browser default or any figure below 120%, blocks of text look cramped, unpleasant and become hard to read.

If we go beyond 150% the gaps become too large and the brain has more difficulty following from one line to the next.

4

Have 45 to 90 characters of text per line

A line of text that is between 45 and 90 characters is the easiest length for people to follow.

If we have a chunk of text and we make each line less than 45 characters long there are too many jumps from one line to the next.

However, if we make lines more than 90 characters it becomes too hard to follow which line comes next.

5

Choose appropriate fonts

Choose a font that reflects the look and feel you want for the website. Experiment with different fonts if necessary.

Consider whether sans-serif, serif or a mixture of both would be best for your site.

Sans-serif: More neutral | Clean | Simple | Modern websites
Serif: Traditional purposes | Storytelling | Long reading

Colours

"Color plays a vitally important role in the world in which we live. Color can sway thinking, change actions, and cause reactions."

1

Use only one base colour

This is a colour not including black, white or grey. I have used #FFB901 (Selective Yellow) as my base colour.

You will probably want to create light and dark versions of your base colour. I have created two light and two dark versions of my base colour.

Too many colours in too many places looks unpleasant and messy which is why we choose one.

2

Use a tool if you need more colours

You may find you need a selection of different colours for your website. If you do, use a tool to select your colour palette.

Using a tool to create your colour palette will ensure you do not use colours that clash or don't look good together.

I like to use Coolors but there are plenty of free tools available.

3

Use colour to draw attention

Colour can be a great way of making people take notice of certain parts of your website.

Not everything on your site is of equal importance. Using colour can highlight the more important parts.

It is a good idea to use colour to accentuate CTAs and buttons.

4

Avoid using black in your design

Black rarely occurs in the real world. The night sky... actually very dark blue. Your black dog... actually very dark brown.

Because of this, using black in your designs can look unnatural and a bit odd. Try a dark grey or other dark colour instead.

The black in my design? Actually a very dark indigo called Space Cadet (#2B2D42).

5

Use colours wisely

Colours can evoke emotional responses in people and have specific associations.

Ever wondered why Twitter, Facebook and LinkedIn all use blue as their base colour? It's because blue gives the impression something is trustworthy.

Red: power and excitement
Orange: cheerful and friendly
Yellow: energetic and happy
Green: nature and harmony
Blue: trustworthy and professional
Purple: nobilty and wealth
Pink: romance and love
Brown: reliability and relaxation

Images

"Images on your website serve much more of a purpose than just making your design look good."

1

You can put text on your images

Text can go directly onto an image provided the contrast between the image and the text is significant enough.

If the contrast is too low the text will be hard to read and your site will fail accessibility tests.

You can check for colour accessibility using this background image a11y check tool.

×

2

Overlay the image

A semi-transparent black overlay is the standard way of doing this. It darkens the image and allows light text to stand out.

You can also a colour to overlay an image. You should use the base colour of your website to ensure it fits with the overall design.

Another way to use an overlay is as a coloured gradient. Consider using a gradient from the dark version of your base colour to the light version of it.

×
×

Overlay the Image

.darken {
    background-image: linear-gradient(rgba(0,0,0,.5), rgba(0,0,0,.5)), url(YOUR IMAGE HERE);
}

This will put a semi-transparent black overlay on top of your background image so light text will be clearly readable even on a light image.

View example on CodePen

3

Put text in a box

If you do not want to overlay your image to get text to stand out, you can use text boxes which sit on the image.

The box should have a slightly transparent background colour so the image can be made out through the box.

Doing so will make the image and the text boxes feel more like a single element.

×
×

Put Text in a Box

.text-box {
    background-color: rgba(0,0,0,.25);
    color: #fff;
    display: inline;
    padding: 10px;
}

This will create a semi-transparent black text box with white text that you can place on a light image.

View example on CodePen

4

Blur the image

This has a similar effect to an overlay and makes the text easier to read.

You can either blur the whole image or put text over a blurred section of an in-focus image.

If placing text over a blurred section of an image, be careful to ensure your text stays on that section on all screen sizes.

×
×

Blur the Image

.image-blur {
    background-image: url(YOUR IMAGE HERE);
    filter: blur(8px);
}

This will blur the background image. Try adjusting the size of the blur to see what looks best with your image.

View example on CodePen

5

The floor fade

This is where we create a gradient overlay that runs from the top to the bottom of an image. The gradient runs from completely transparent to semi-transparent black.

As the image fades towards black at the bottom, light colour text is placed on the darker section.

When done well the floor fade can be almost unnoticeable.

×
×

The Floor Fade

.floor-fade {
    background-image: linear-gradient(to bottom, rgba(0,0,0,0), rgba(0,0,0,.6)), url(YOUR IMAGE HERE);
}

This will darken the background image towards the bottom. Light text can then be placed on the darkened section.

View example on CodePen

Icons

"Icons can quickly communicate meaning without the need for text. They are visual metaphors people can relate to."

1

Listing features and steps

If you are working on a software product website, icons can be a great way of illustrating the product's features.

If your site has setup or sign up steps, icons can work well to keep text explanations shorter.

In each case, icons will help draw the eye of your user to these important parts of your site.

×

2

User actions and links

Icons are particularly useful for navigation. There are two rules you should follow:

First, icons should be recognisable. If it is not obvious what your icon means, choose another.

Second, label your icons if you have room. Whilst a label isn't essential it will remove any ambiguity, should it exist.

×

3

Icons should not take centre stage

Whilst important and useful, icons should only have a supporting role on your website.

You shouldn't use them as the main part of your site. Too many icons can make it cluttered and confusing.

If you use too many, the icons can lose their meaning and impact.

×

4

Use icon fonts or SVGs

Icon fonts and SVGs are vector images. Vector images scale perfectly. This means no matter how big they are they will remain sharp.

Standard images (e.g. png, jpeg) will become blurred as they enlarge.

SVGs and icon fonts are also much more easily manipulated with CSS.

Spacing & Layout

"Spacing makes things clearer, cleaner and crisper. There are three aspects to consider: white space, line spacing and padding."

1

Use white space for clarity

White space refers to empty space on your website (which don't actually need to be white). Empty space may seem wasteful but actually it helps users.

Content with space around it looks more appealing and is easier to take in. You should look to space out elements, groups of elements and sections.

However, be careful not to exaggerate the gaps otherwise your site will start to look disjointed.

×

2

Define the hierarchy

White space describes invisible relationships between the elements of your website. You can help define the heirarchy of your site by using white space to separate sections.

You should define what you want you audience to see first and then establish a flow that corresponds to yur content's message. You can use white space to build that flow.

When well thought out white space will create distinct sections flowing from one to the next.

Getting Inspired

"Imitation is an effective driver of creativity, even for experts."

1

Collect designs you like

Even small projects will benefit from you spending an hour or two looking for inspiration. Start by simply bookmarking websites you like whilst browing the web and use them as a source of inspiration for your projects.

Many designers will go to sites such as Dribbble or Behance to view the work of other designers. This will help get their creative juices flowing. It will also help you stay up to date with design trends.

Less obvious places to look for design inspiration are design podcasts, outdated print design manuals, food / drink packaging, movie title sequences and simply by taking a step outside and looking at what's around you... posters, billboards, shop signs and nature itself.

×

2

Understand the designs

When you find websites you like, don't be content with simply liking them. Try to understand them.

Ask yourself... Why do these websites look good? What is it about them that I like? Considering what your collection of inspirational websites have in common may help you answer these questions.

You should also try to understand the technical side of the websites you like. Inspect the code and see how they have been built in HTML and CSS.

×

3

Steal like an artist

Examples from art history like Pablo Picasso and Vincent Van Gogh, who created their artwork through active imitation of others' artworks, show even the best artists and designers imitate others.

When you first start out in design, simply make your designs look like sites you like. You do not need to worry about being original or having your own style.

Originality and forming your own style will come automatically as you get better and more experienced as a designer.

×

Conversion

"Getting traffic to your website is great, but if that traffic doesnt convert, its almost useless."

1

Build trust

Building trust with your visitors is important if you want their visit to convert to a sale / sign up etc.

Rather than trying to sell to your visitor right away, you can offer them something free and useful. A fairly standard give away item is an ebook or a free tutorial / short course.

Providing something useful for free will make visitors trust your paid services and they are more likely to convert to paying customers.

×

2

Repeat your primary action

On most websites you will find a call to action (CTA). A primary CTA is generally something like a "Sign Up" button.

To encourage users to click your CTA you should make it highly visible and easy to access. This means placing it in more than one prominent place on your website.

Standard places to put your primary CTA are your navbar, above the fold header and at the end of the page.

×

3

Grab attention

You do not have long to make a good enough impression to convince a user to stay on your website. A reasonable estimate is about 10 seconds. As such you need to grab their attention and do it quickly!

Popup boxes are a great way to grab a visitor's attention and encourage them to take an action. For example, sign up to your newsletter.

Whilst popup boxes are almost universally regarded as a bit annoying, they are proven to work. If you use a popup to encourage users to take an action they are more likely to do so than if the popup were not there.

×

4

Explain the benefits

If you want your website visitors to carry out an action, tell them what the benefit of that action will be.

Rather than writing "Click Here" or "Sign Up" as your call to action, try saying something like "Try for Free" instead.

Using language like this will increase the likelihood of the user clicking the button. They know the benefit of carrying out the action... they will get to try what they're interested in for free!

×

5

Limit required inputs

A sure way to discourage users from completing a sign up process is to ask them for too much information. Only ask for the information you actually need.

If you must ask for more information, make as many fields as possible optional. You can easily ask for more later.

×

6

Use social proof

Social proof can take the form of client testimonials, user reviews, subscriber counts and similar stats.

Including this type of information to your visitors will inspire confidence in your product or service.

It makes your site more credible, can explain specific benefits and offers unbiased evidence of positive user experiences.

×

7

Create urgency and scarcity

Creating a sense of urgency or scarcity will encourage website visitors to make a purchase.

You can create a sense of urgency by making something time dependent. For example if you are selling an online course you can show a price reduction that is only valid for 48 hours.

If you have a tangible products, you can include your stock numbers to show there is not many items left of a particular product. For examle, "Only 2 items left... order now!"
