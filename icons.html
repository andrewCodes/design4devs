Icons

"Icons can quickly communicate meaning without the need for text. They are visual metaphors people can relate to."

1

Listing features and steps

If you are working on a software product website, icons can be a great way of illustrating the product's features.

If your site has setup or sign up steps, icons can work well to keep text explanations shorter.

In each case, icons will help draw the eye of your user to these important parts of your site.

2

User actions and links

Icons are particularly useful for navigation. There are two rules you should follow:

First, icons should be recognisable. If it is not obvious what your icon means, choose another.

Second, label your icons if you have room. Whilst a label isn't essential it will remove any ambiguity, should it exist.

3

Icons should not take centre stage

Whilst important and useful, icons should only have a supporting role on your website.

You shouldn't use them as the main part of your site. Too many icons can make it cluttered and confusing.

If you use too many, the icons can lose their meaning and impact.

4

Use icon fonts or SVGs

Icon fonts and SVGs are vector images. Vector images scale perfectly. This means no matter how big they are they will remain sharp.

Standard images (e.g. png, jpeg) will become blurred as they enlarge.

SVGs and icon fonts are also much more easily manipulated with CSS.
