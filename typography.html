Typography

"The art and technique of arranging type to make written language readable and beautiful"

1

Use a font size between 15 and 25 pixels

For body text we need to make sure that our text is neither too small nor too large.

If we make our body text any smaller than 15 pixels becomes too hard to read. As a result of this, your text will fail accessibility tests and your site's search engine ranking will fall.

If we make body text any larger than 25 pixels it looks out of place.

2

Use really big font sizes for headings

There really is no size limit here. We are looking to grab the reader's attention.

When using very large font sizes you may find letters become blocky, too bold and don't look great. To address this, try reducing the font-weight.

You can give large letters a little more breathing space by increasing the letter-spacing.

3

Use line-spacing between 120% and 150%

Browser default line-spacing is 100%. However, this is too small for blocks of text.

If we use the browser default or any figure below 120%, blocks of text look cramped, unpleasant and become hard to read.

If we go beyond 150% the gaps become too large and the brain has more difficulty following from one line to the next.

4

Have 45 to 90 characters of text per line

A line of text that is between 45 and 90 characters is the easiest length for people to follow.

If we have a chunk of text and we make each line less than 45 characters long there are too many jumps from one line to the next.

However, if we make lines more than 90 characters it becomes too hard to follow which line comes next.

5

Choose appropriate fonts

Choose a font that reflects the look and feel you want for the website. Experiment with different fonts if necessary.

Consider whether sans-serif, serif or a mixture of both would be best for your site.

Sans-serif: More neutral | Clean | Simple | Modern websites
Serif: Traditional purposes | Storytelling | Long reading
